Stepping Over Common Functions

Modern C++ programs make frequent use of smart pointers and STL. Most of the time, given a statement like p->func(); where p is a smart pointer, you may want to step inside function func rather than p's operator->().

To this purpose, the contextual menu entry Always Step Over... may be used. The first time the debugger steps inside operator->() code, use the options under this menu to disable future stepping inside the function.
